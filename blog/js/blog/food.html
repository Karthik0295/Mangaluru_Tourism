Top delicacies of Mangalore

The cuisine of Mangalore is influenced by the region's unique blend of cultures, including Hindu, Muslim, and Christian. Here are some of the top foods to try in Mangalore:

Explore

1. Neer Dosa

Probably one of the most versatile dishes to come out of south India, a good dosa is relished by people all over the country. From the popular masala dosa to the rava variation and everything in-between, dosas are universally loved. And Mangaluru has its own version of this classic dish. Neer dosa, a delicacy popular in the Tulu region, makes for a great breakfast option. Neer dosa in Tulu literally translates to 'water dosa'. So the dish essentially derives its name from the watery consistency of the batter. Usually accompanied with coconut chutney, neer dosa also goes well with coconut milk and even sagu.

2. Kori Rotti

Mangaloreans love their spice. There are no two ways about it. And the chilli-based kori sukka hits all the right chords in this respect. Also known as kori ghassi, this Mangalorean chicken curry is creamy, spicy with an overload of the goodness of coconut. A quintessential Tuluva recipe, kori rotti is a combination of chicken curry and crispy dry wafers made using boiled rice. 3. Mangalore Buns

For the uninitiated, Mangalore buns are an Indianised version of sweet donuts. Typically a banana poori, these puffed buns are an all-day snack. Thick and flaky, they get a mild sweetness from the ripe bananas. Fermentation plays an essential role in the making of the batter and lends the soft and pliable texture to the dough. Normally served with coconut chutney and sambar, in some regions it is also accompanied with potato palya and vegetable kurma.

4. Chicken Sukka

Chicken sukka, kori sukka or kori ajadina, call it what you may, this dry chicken curry is for those whose love for spice knows no bounds. Made using morsels of chicken, the preparation is native to the Konkan coast. Fusing a great host of flavours including the tanginess of tamarind, the sweetness of coconut and fieriness of red chillies, the distinct coastal flavour packs a punch.

5. Gudbad Icecream

Gadbad ice cream is a popular dessert in Mangalore, and it's a must-try for anyone visiting the city. Gadbad literally means "confusion," and the name comes from the fact that the dessert is a mixture of various flavors of ice cream and fruits, topped with nuts and cherries. The traditional Gadbad ice cream consists of three layers of ice cream, usually vanilla, chocolate, and strawberry, topped with chopped fruits like bananas, apples, and grapes, and garnished with nuts and cherries. The ice cream is usually served in a tall glass, and it's a perfect dessert to cool off on a hot day.

6. Goli Baje

Goli baje, also known as Mangalore bajji, is a popular snack from Mangalore, a coastal city in Karnataka, India. It's a type of fritter made with a batter of flour, yogurt, and spices, and deep-fried until golden brown and crispy on the outside. The name "Goli baje" comes from the Kannada word "goli," which means ball, and "baje," which means fritter. The snack is usually served with coconut chutney and a cup of tea, and it's a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

7. Moode

Moode is a traditional dish from Mangalore, a coastal city in the state of Karnataka in India. It's a type of steamed rice cake that's made using rice flour, grated coconut, and spices. Moode is a popular breakfast and snack item in Mangalore, and it's often served with chutney, sambar, or fish curry. The dish is popular among locals in Mangalore, and it's also gaining popularity in other parts of the country.

8. Chicken Ghee Roast

Fiery, tangy and laced with ghee, as the name clearly suggests, chicken ghee roast is iconic for all the right reasons. Health freaks out there, you might want to ditch your diet for a short while just for this one. The origins of this dish can be traced back to Kundapura, a small coastal town in Karnataka. Though the dish has nothing to do with roasting, its unique aroma can be credited to the unusual mix of dry spices. While the Chettinad version of this delicacy is also extremely popular, for us the Managalorean Bunt variation is hands down an all-time favourite.
