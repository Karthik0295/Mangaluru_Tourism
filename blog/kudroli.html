Kudroli Gokarnanatha Temple

History: The Kudroli Gokarnath Temple, Mangalore, was built in the year 1912. However, the temple as seen today came into existence in 1991, when it was renovated and expanded. Since the year 1989, the temple has played a pivotal role in the celebration of the Dasara festival in Mangalore in a grand manner. The Kudroli Gokarnanatha Temple was originally built for Shiva in keeping with the Nath tradition that was prevalent in the region. Nath traditions were practised by a sub-sect within Hinduism, the tradition combined the tenets of Buddhism, Shaivism, and Hatha Yoga. The Nath tradition was founded by Matsyendranath. The architecture of the Kudroli Gokarnanatha Temple is a blend of the Kerala and Dravidian styles, with intricate carvings and beautiful paintings adorning its walls. The temple has a towering entrance gate and a large prayer hall where devotees can offer their prayers to Lord Shiva.

As you enter the main gate of Kudroli Gokarnath Temple, a wide-open- courtyard leads towards the main Gopuram.

  • The outer courtyard of Gokarnanatheshwara Temple has beautiful temples dedicated to Hanuman ( Bhagwan Hanuman Mandir ).

  • Shirdi Sai Baba Temple ( Shirdi Sai Baba Mandir )

  • A massive Nandi stands vigil facing the main entrance to the temple.

  • A beautiful black statue of Lord Shiva with his consort Parvati and children Ganesha and Ayyappa can be seen behind the Nandi statue.

  • There is a beautiful marble statue of Shri Narayana Guru near the entrance to the temple.

During the 10 day Mangalore Dasara celebrations, the city is decorated with vibrant and colourful lights. During Navratri, the Swarna Kalamantapa within the Shree Gokarnanatheshwara Kshetra comes alive with festivities. Goddess Sharada idol, along with idols of Mahaganapathi and the Navadurgas are installed. The Navadurgas idols are those of Shailaputri, Brahmacharini, Chandraghanta, Kushmandaini, Skandamata, Katyayini, Kaalratri, Mahagauri, and Siddhidatri. Pilikula Nisarga Dhama, Tannirbhavi Beach, Panambur Beach are other attractions to visit within a few kms from Manjunatha temple.

How To reach Kudroli Gokarnanatha Temple ?

  • Kudroli Gokarnanatha Temple also referred to as Shree Gokarnanatha Kshetra is located in Mangalore City in the southern Indian state of Karnataka

  • The Kudroli Gokarnanatheshwara temple is about 12 kilometres from the Mangaluru International Airport

  • The Kudroli temple is about 2.7 kilometres from the Mangalore railway station

  • The Kudroli Gokarnanatheeshwara temple is about 3.4 kilometres from the KSRTC Bus Stand in Mangalore

  • Look out for Kudroli Dwara Of Gokarnanatha Temple
